---
name: Voice and Tone
description: Enforce the subreddit's voice — warm, obsessive, not corporate
---

r/factorymustgrow sounds like someone who just optimized their main bus at 2am and can't stop talking about it. The energy is genuine obsession — the person who stays up too late because they found one more bottleneck. Direct, warm, nerdy, never corporate.

The voice standards:

1. **Warm obsession over polish.** Write like you're explaining something cool to a friend, not writing copy. Enthusiasm is good. Marketing enthusiasm is not.
2. **Direct, no gatekeeping.** Spaghetti factories are factories too. No elitism about "real" factory design — Factorio or software. Everyone starts somewhere.
3. **Technical specificity over vague claims.** "I reduced my build time from 12 minutes to 90 seconds by parallelizing the test stage" beats "I made my pipeline faster."
4. **No marketing speak.** No "leverage," "synergize," "empower," "solutions." Talk like an engineer, not a landing page. If it could appear in a LinkedIn post, rewrite it.
5. **Factory language is native.** Throughput, bottleneck, main bus, blueprint, spaghetti, megabase, production line — this vocabulary is natural here, not forced metaphor.

Flag as failing if:

- Content uses corporate/marketing language ("leverage," "synergize," "empower," "solution," "unlock")
- Tone is condescending toward beginners or less-optimized builds
- Content reads like a press release or product announcement rather than community discussion
- Voice is detached or formal where it should be enthusiastic and specific
- Gatekeeping language that implies some factories don't count

Do NOT flag:

- Casual tone, slang, or Factorio-specific jargon
- Strong opinions about factory design (passion is welcome, elitism is not)
- Rough or unpolished writing — authenticity over polish
- Technical depth that might be hard for newcomers (specificity is a feature)
