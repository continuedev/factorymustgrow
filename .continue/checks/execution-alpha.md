---
name: Execution Alpha
description: Enforce the core ethos — share the blueprint, not just the screenshot
---

r/factorymustgrow's core value proposition is execution alpha: the specific decisions, configs, workflows, and patterns that give builders an edge. The sub's value compounds when people open-source their factory designs, not just show the output. "Look what I built" is welcome. "Here's how I built it and why it works" is what makes the sub special.

The execution alpha standards:

1. **Blueprints over screenshots.** The highest-value content shares reproducible knowledge — configs, architecture diagrams, decision rationale, specific numbers. A screenshot of a working factory is good. The blueprint string is better.
2. **Concrete over abstract.** "Use CI/CD" is noise. "Here's my exact GitHub Actions workflow that deploys to three environments with canary releases, and here's why I chose this over ArgoCD" is signal.
3. **Share the why, not just the what.** The decision is more valuable than the result. Why this belt layout? Why this pipeline architecture? Why this tool over the alternatives? The reasoning is the alpha.
4. **Failures are alpha too.** Post-mortems, failed experiments, and "here's what I tried that didn't work" are high-value content. Knowing what doesn't work saves everyone time.
5. **The sub's value compounds.** Each shared blueprint makes the next builder faster. Content should be written to be useful to someone building a similar factory, not just to show off.

Flag as failing if:

- Content is pure showcase with no reproducible detail (screenshot-only posts with no explanation)
- Advice is generic enough to appear in any tech subreddit ("just use Docker")
- Content hoards knowledge — teases results without sharing the approach
- Posts that are pure opinion without supporting evidence or experience
- Content that treats the sub as a portfolio rather than a knowledge base

Do NOT flag:

- Beginner posts that share what they learned, even if the execution is basic (alpha is relative)
- Questions and help requests (asking "how do I optimize this?" invites alpha from others)
- Spaghetti posts that own it — "this is messy but it works, here's why" is valid alpha
- Posts that share partial blueprints (some alpha is better than no alpha)
- Humor and memes that riff on factory culture (not every post needs to be a tutorial)
