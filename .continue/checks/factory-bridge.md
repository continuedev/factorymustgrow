---
name: Factory Bridge
description: Enforce the cross-community bridge between Factorio and software factories
---

r/factorymustgrow exists at the intersection of two communities: Factorio players who think in throughput and software engineers who build automated systems. Neither side dominates. Content that connects the two worlds is highest value — the insight that a Factorio main bus is a CI/CD pipeline, that belt balancers are load balancers, that biters are production incidents.

The bridge standards:

1. **Both factories welcome.** Pure Factorio content and pure software factory content are both on-topic. Neither needs to justify its presence.
2. **Bridge content is highest value.** Posts that draw connections between game factories and software factories — shared patterns, analogous problems, transferable insights — are the sub's unique contribution.
3. **No gatekeeping either direction.** "This isn't really about Factorio" and "this isn't really about software" are both violations. If it's about building, optimizing, or scaling systems, it belongs.
4. **Shared vocabulary is an asset.** Terms like throughput, bottleneck, main bus, spaghetti, blueprint, and megabase are native to both worlds. Content should lean into this shared language, not away from it.

Flag as failing if:

- Content explicitly excludes one side (e.g., "this sub is for Factorio only" or "keep the game stuff out")
- Moderation or community standards favor one community over the other
- Bridge metaphors are dismissed as "just an analogy" rather than engaged with as genuine insight
- Content creates a hierarchy where one type of factory is more legitimate than the other

Do NOT flag:

- Posts that are purely about Factorio or purely about software — both are welcome
- Leaning more heavily on one side's terminology in a specific post (natural for the topic)
- Disagreements about whether a specific analogy holds (debate is healthy, gatekeeping is not)
- Content from adjacent factory games (Satisfactory, Dyson Sphere Program, Shapez) — all factories welcome
